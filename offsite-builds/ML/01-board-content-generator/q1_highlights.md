# Q1 2026 Key Highlights & Context

## Strategic Wins
- **RLUSD hit $1.5B market cap** in under 14 months - fastest-growing institutional stablecoin. Velocity metrics confirm genuine commercial usage, not just speculative holding.
- **UK FCA and Luxembourg CSSF licenses granted** (January 2026) - opens EU passporting and UK institutional market. AMINA Bank became first European bank on end-to-end Ripple Payments solution.
- **Hidden Road integration ahead of schedule** - cross-margining between digital and traditional assets in beta with 5 institutional clients. RLUSD as collateral on prime brokerage is a differentiated offering no competitor can match.
- **XRP ETF momentum** - spot XRP ETFs have absorbed $1.37B with 35+ consecutive days of net inflows. Institutional on-ramp expanding.
- **SBI Holdings partnership deepening** - MOU signed for RLUSD cross-border settlements in Japan. SBI also issued $65M on-chain bond on XRPL.

## Challenges & Risks
- **XRP price correction** - down ~62% from July 2025 ATH of $3.65 to ~$1.37. Impacts custody AUM fees and narrative, though not core business revenue.
- **Integration costs running above plan** - GTreasury cloud migration and Rail license transfers driving $15M expense overage. Expected to normalize in Q2.
- **ODL corridor utilization at 67% vs 75% target** - only ~40% of RippleNet partners actively use XRP for settlement. Converting messaging-only partners remains the central adoption challenge.
- **Cross-margin adoption at 28% vs 35% target** - Hidden Road's cross-margining product is powerful but new. Institutional clients moving cautiously.
- **SWIFT + Chainlink partnership** - SWIFT piloting cross-chain interoperability that could bridge TradFi/DeFi without Ripple. Not an immediate threat but validates alternative approaches.

## Competitive Developments
- JPMorgan Kinexys opened to non-JPM banks for first time (12 regional APAC banks)
- Circle opened Dubai office, pursuing DFSA license (competing in Ripple's strategic ME market)
- SWIFT GPI now settles 72% within 30 minutes (narrowing speed gap)
- Mastercard hedging between RLUSD and USDC for card settlement pilots

## Board-Level Decisions Needed
- **London office expansion** - at 94% capacity. Evaluating Canary Wharf options. Estimated cost: $2-4M.
- **Q2 hiring plan** - 42 open reqs ($12M annualized cost). Biggest areas: engineering (18), sales (12).
- **GTreasury cloud migration budget increase** - requesting additional $3M to complete migration by Q2 end.

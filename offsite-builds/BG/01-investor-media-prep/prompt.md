# Investor & Media Prep

## Prompt

I'm CEO of Ripple. I have a steady cadence of high-stakes external engagements — investor calls, media interviews, conference keynotes, policy meetings, and partner conversations. Each one requires different framing, different talking points, and awareness of different landmines. Prepping for these currently means my team and I manually pull together context from multiple sources, and it's never as thorough as I want.

Using my connected sources (Gmail, Calendar, Google Drive) — or the attached `upcoming_engagements.json` as fallback — build me a prep packet for each upcoming external engagement.

### For Each Engagement, Generate:

**1. Context Brief**
- Who am I meeting? Background on the person/organization, their relationship with Ripple, and what they care about.
- What happened last time? Summary of our most recent interaction (from email, notes, or CRM).
- What's their current narrative? What are they saying publicly about crypto, payments, or Ripple specifically?

**2. Key Talking Points** (3-5 per engagement)
Based on recent Ripple developments, tailor talking points to what this audience cares about. An investor wants growth metrics and competitive moats. A journalist wants the story angle. A regulator wants compliance and consumer protection.

**3. Anticipated Questions & Suggested Responses**
The 3 hardest questions they're likely to ask, with suggested responses that are honest, specific, and don't create new headlines.

**4. Landmines to Avoid**
Topics or framings to steer away from. This could be ongoing negotiations, sensitive competitive dynamics, or topics where our position is evolving.

**5. Supporting Materials**
Links or references to recent press releases, data points, or documents I should review beforehand. Flag if anything needs updating.

### Output Format
One section per engagement, in chronological order. Each section should fit on one page. Use bold for key names, numbers, and dates. Make it printable.

---

## Stretch Goals

1. **Post-engagement follow-up drafter** — After each meeting, give Claude a quick debrief ("they asked about X, committed to Y") and have it draft a follow-up email.
2. **Media monitoring summary** — Before media engagements, pull a summary of the last 30 days of Ripple media coverage with sentiment analysis.
3. **Competitive narrative tracker** — Track how our competitors frame themselves vs. how we frame ourselves, and identify messaging gaps.

# BG — CEO

## Survey Response
- **Tools interested in:** Claude Cowork
- **Tasks to hand off to AI:**
  1. Morning synthesis across all business units — performance, issues, decisions needed — in one place
  2. Prep for investor meetings, media appearances, and policy conversations
  3. Track strategic priorities across the company and surface what's stalled or off-track

## Session Build

### Primary: Exercise 00 — CEO Strategic Briefing
**Complexity:** Medium-Hard | **Estimated time:** 35-45 min (first briefing in ~12 min, then refine)

Builds a cross-company strategic briefing that synthesizes business unit performance, pending decisions, external signals (regulatory, competitive, market), and stakeholder prep into a single morning read. This is the "meta" version of the daily briefing — where every other exec synthesizes across their function, BG needs synthesis across all functions.

Uses connected sources (Gmail, Calendar, Slack, Google Drive) or sample data as fallback. The sample data includes a BU performance summary, CEO-level inbox highlights, and an upcoming stakeholder calendar.

### Optional Deep-Dives (Stretch / Follow-Up)

#### 1. Investor & Media Prep
Takes upcoming investor meetings, media appearances, and policy engagements and generates contextual briefing packets: key talking points, anticipated questions, recent coverage to reference, and landmines to avoid.

#### 2. Strategic Priorities Tracker
A cross-company dashboard tracking Ripple's top strategic priorities — acquisitions, product launches, regulatory milestones, partnerships — with status, owners, blockers, and staleness detection.

## Facilitator Notes
- **Support level:** Self-directed with light check-ins. BG sets the tone for the room — if he's engaged, everyone follows.
- **Key insight:** The CEO briefing is the highest-leverage version of the "Synthesize My Chaos" theme. It should feel like the ultimate proof of concept — if this works for the CEO, it works for everyone.
- **Approach:** Let him explore. He'll likely have opinions about what matters and what doesn't — encourage him to iterate on the priority logic. The refinement loop is the real value.
- **If he finishes early:** Point him at the Investor & Media Prep (Exercise 01). High-stakes external engagements are where AI prep saves the most time.
- **Room dynamics:** BG's engagement level will influence the energy. Mike should check in periodically but not hover.
